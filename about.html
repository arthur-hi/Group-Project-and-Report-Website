What is Cyber Security?


Cybersecurity is the collective measures and technologies employed to protect digital systems, networks, devices, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, or damage.
It’s a critical shield in our increasingly digital world, safeguarding personal and financial information, intellectual property, and even national security against a growing array of cyber threats.

The key concepts of cybersecurity revolve around the C.I.A.
While the acronym C.I.A. is often used to represent the Central Intelligence Agency, in the context of cybersecurity, it stands for Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.

Confidentiality involves protecting data from unauthorized access
Integrity ensures the accuracy and completeness of data.
Availability guarantees that data and resources are accessible to authorized users when needed.

Why is it Important?


Cybersecurity is crucial because it encompasses the strategies and measures put in place to safeguard not only our computers and networks but also the invaluable data they house.
It’s an all-encompassing shield that defends against unauthorized access and threats lurking in the vast digital landscape.

It ensures that sensitive information, such as personal details, health records, intellectual property, and trade secrets, is kept safe from theft, exposure, or manipulation by malicious actors.
Moreover, as our reliance on technology grows, so does the potential impact of cyber threats.

Types of Cyber Threats


  • Phishing: The art of deception to steal sensitive information.
  • Ransomware: Malicious software that holds data hostage for ransom.
  • Spyware: Software that secretly monitors and gathers information.
  • Ransomware: Malicious software that encrypts a victim’s files and demands payment for the decryption key.
  • Phishing Attacks: Deceptive attempts to steal sensitive information like login credentials and credit card numbers through fake emails or websites.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks: Overloading a system’s resources to disrupt service and make it unavailable to legitimate users.
